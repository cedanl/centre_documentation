Intro

CEDA stands for Centre for Educational Data Analytics (of in goed Nederlands: Centrum voor Educatieve Data Analyse). It is a project within the pilot hub ‘Studiedata & AI’ (Educational data & AI) of the Npuls programme. This document is for the basics to get working within CEDA. In order to be succinct the document is written in directive language, however, the intention is to support and enable everyone to spend time on the fun things. Many of the guidelines are derived from SURF or the Npuls programme.

General

  • Working in this project is done through detachment. You get a detachment contract send to you by the HR Support (Flex). For any questions regarding this type of contracts, consult with them first. If there is still confusion, cc the project lead.

  • SURF is the lead party of Npuls, therefore, many basics go through SURF environments. Access to these basics is given via your SURF account.

  • When logging into SURF environments, eduVPN is often necessary. Without eduVPN, the site seems to be inaccessible. Login in with Institutional access and then select SURF as institution.

  • As part of the Npuls program, there is a request to fill in hours via SAM. This is done with your SURF account.

    • When we all have SURF access a member will demonstrate it to other users. A more instruction can be find within the SURF Sharepoint.

    • You only have to add the hours you actually work, so no holidays or sick days (that still goes via your regular employer). Within your detachement there is a number of hours (often 8). When you put in more hours on average please consult with the project lead how to decrease your workload.

  • Progress and communication is by default doen through JIRA and Teams and also goes via your SURF account, ask the project lead for an invite link to the ‘Studiedata team’ within the larger SURF environment. You can also have a look at the SURF intranet, the Npuls area there and wiki.

  • They might ask if you want a laptop for your work at Npuls. Since most of you will only work 8 hours per week for less than a year, this doesn’t make much sense. The project lead has no SURF laptop either.

Within CEDA

  • We work mainly within an open source github project where you get write-permissions. Let someone know if you want access on a specific account. (PS: the name ed2c refers to the old working title of ceda).

    • You can use any local git client, for Smartgit there is some working knowledge and documentation available, but Github Desktop and GitKraken are also used widely. In addition, you can work directly from the command-line with Git if you prefer.

    • IDE’s often also have a (limited) internal git client.

    • For guidelines on commits see the ways of working document.

    • The project lead will invite you all on your institutional emailadress (so you keep access after your project is finished). If you prefer another emailadress, let the project lead know.

  • Within the pilot hub Study Data & AI, we keep track of our work on a Kanban Board. Again, for guidelines on the usage of Kanban, see the ways of working document.

Collaboration

  • Everyone has a sub-project where that person is in the lead. However, as you know, team work makes a dream work so collaboration / meeting across sub-projects are encouraged. In addition, there are CEDA interal meetings and workshops.

  • The proposed monthly schedule for collaboration is as follows:

    • Week 1 - Face-to-face meeting at SURF in Utrecht for a half-day including lunch

    • Week 2 - Online stand-up for sharing progress, hold-ups and requests (<30 minutes)

    • Week 3 - One-on-one with the project lead if deemed beneficial

    • Week 4 - Online stand-up for sharing progress, hold-ups and requests (<30 minutes)

  • The proposed tool for asynchronous contact with each other within the project is Teams

  • Does anyone have other preferences or suggestions regarding the schedule or the tool?

First Steps

  • See the ways of working document for the general expectations. Since we work agile there is no need to plan your whole project in detail.

    • SUGGESTION: make a document of maximal 1 A4 with in bullet points:

      • the minimum viable product you want to achieve

      • when you want to have a first workshop or working group meeting?

      • Three ‘stories’ (a story is approximately a day work) with the specific first steps

  • In addition, not everything has to be done (perfect). The most important ‘business’ goal is to get stuff working and share this with other data professionals in education to see if it works for them. Of course, to do this properly structure within your project and code helps.
